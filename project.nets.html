The Brookyln Nets

The Brooklyn Nets are a professional basketball team based in Brooklyn, New York. The Brooklyn Nets went through multiple location changes, going from the New York Nets to the New Jersey Nets before finally moving to Brooklyn, New York. The Nets play their home games at the beautiful Barclays Center. Over the course of its franchise, the Nets established themselves as a pretty mediocre team, peaking at a win rate of .634 until they assembled a superteam in Kevin Durant, Kyrie Irving, James Harden, Blake Griffin, Joe Harris, and to put the cherry on top, all-time great Steve Nash as head coach. So far, despite their big 3 always being fragmented due to injuries or some kind of Kyrie business, their team has excelled, establishing themselves as a force to be reckoned with in the NBA.

Looking at the graph above, we can come to the conclusion that the Nets are overall a historically mediocre team, at times reaching about a .500 win rate but also dropping to as low as a .146 win rate. The Brooklyn nets were referred to as the New Jersey Nets until 2021, when they relocated to their current base, Brooklyn. The most notable player of the Nets in the early 1990s was Derrick Coleman, averinging a solid 19[points], 3[rebounds], and 5[assists] in the 1991 season. Kenny Anderson and Drazen Petrovic were also solid players during the 90s decade, however despite having these three all stars on tehir team, they only managed to make the playoffs four times in the 1990s. Each time they lost in the Conference Quarterfinals. Other notable pickups in this decade include Jayson Williams, Stephon Marbury, and Keith Van Horn. During the 1998-99 season, after a 3-17 start, head coach John Calipari was fired. The 2000s were the years where the Nets would see thei brightest and darkest moments. Starting with the bright moments, Stephon Marbury established himself as an all star caliber player. However, he would later be traded for Jason Kidd, who was deemed a better fit for the Nets offense. Kenyon Martin, along with Jason Kidd and Keith Van Horn, would lead the Nets to their best season in history, going 52-30 in the 2001-02 season. They couldn't put the cherry on top with a championship victory, losing to the superteam that was the Los Angeles Lakers. The next season, they would build off of that, acquiring all time great Dikembe Mutombo. They kept their position at the 1st seed in the Eastern Conference. They even managed to make the NBA Finals again but unfortunately they lost to the dominant San Antonio Spurs. The next season, the 2003-04 season, the Detroit Pistons would put a stop to their chances at a third straight Finals appearance. The Nets would never make another Finals (until 2021?). Prime Richard Jefferson emerged from this however, so it wasn't too bad for Nets fans.

The Downfall

Over the course of the next years, the Nets would acquire superstar Vince Carter but the team would never return to their former successes of their Finals appearances. For some reason, the Nets traded two of their superstars for first round picks and mediocre role players. To end the decade, the Nets ended 12-70, the worst in franchise history and 6th worst in NBA history.

A Glimpse of Hope?

The 2010s were a decent period for the Nets. Notable players that the Nets acquired during this period include: Deron Williams, Joe Johnson, and lastly two members of the Celtic's championship winning trio, Kevin Garnett and Paul Pierce. In addition, a familiar face showed up as head coach: Jason Kidd. The Nets would make the playoffs during the 2012-13 season and the 2013-14 season until they traded Garnett to his original team, the Minnesota Timberwolves. Despite not having Garnett, the Nets excelled and made the playoffs again the next season, largely due to the efforts of Deron Williams. However, after these successes, the Nets struggled once more after Deron Williams left. In addition, their previously acquired players had either left or were injured. The only highlight players after this period were Brook Lopez, Jarett Allen, and D'Angelo Russel. These failures did not last long; out of nowhere, Nets players whom no one even heard of started performing exceedingly well, including Spencer Dinwidde, Caris Levert, Jarrett Allen, and more. Sadly, after the successes that came weith this miracle team, they were traded in order to construct the super team we know and love(?) today: Kevin Durant, James Harden, Joe Harris, Blake Griffin, and DeAndre Jordan.

The Nets have been a historically average team, however that could definitely change with this crazy super team.
