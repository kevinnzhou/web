The Brookyln Nets

The New York Knicks are a professional basketball team based in Manhattan, New York. Their home games are played in Madison Square Garden, one of the most iconic entertainment institutions in New York City. Founded by Ned Irish in 1946, the Knicks were pretty dominant in the 1950s, making 3 consecutive Finals.

The graph above shows the stead decline of the New York Knicks from the 1990s to the present day. During this time period, the Knicks peaked in the 1992-93 season, led by Hall of Famer Patrick Ewing, John Starks, and Charles Smith. This trio along with the rest of their solid team managed to make the ECF (Eastern Conference Finals only to lose to Michael Jordan's legendary Chicago Bulls. After Jordan's untimely retirement though, the Knicks redeemed themselves the next season but lost in the Finals to the Houston Rockets, led by Hakeem Olajuwon. After these Finals-years, the Knicks would not make another Finals until the 1998-99 season, where they would lose to Tim Duncan and his San Antonio Spurs. This would be the last time they would ever make the Finals. From that season forawrd, they went on to make the ECF one more time before making only 6 playoffs from the 2000-01 season forward.

The Downfall

The downfall of the New York Knicks began with the resignation of Head Coach Jeff Van Gundy during the 2001-02 season, leading the Knicks to their first season in which they missed the playoffs since the 1987-88 season. For some reason, the Knicks opted to sign older, washed-up veterans rather than investing in younger talents. In addition, some of their core players were very injury-prone, resulting in more chaos for the Knicks. When Isiah Thomas was elected as the President of the Knicks, he organized many trades, bringing players like Stephon Marbury and Channing Frye to the team. They finally ended their playoff drought, but were swept by the New Jersey Nets. After being accused of sexual harassment, Isiah Thomas was replaced by Donnie h, who set a goal of rebuilding the Knicks to their former glory. Notable changes include appointing Mike D'Antoni as head coach and aquiring players such as David Lee, Chris Wilcox, Amar'e Stoudamire, and Darko Miličić (however Darko was not very well-liked).

The imfamous James Dolan and Donnie Walsh acquired Amare Stoudamire for a whopping $99.7 million 5-year contract, and the iconic Denver Nuggerst star Carmelo Anthony. Carmelo led the Knicks to their first season about .500 W/L % since 2001. The Knicks struggled after the 2011 NBA Lockout due to miscommunications on offense. Carmelo Anthony was also criticized for his ball-dominant play style. Then, the iconic Jeremy Lin emerged from the bench. The historic Linsanity period resulted in the Knicks returning to a win percentage of above .500. Lin led the offense like no one else on the Knicks. However, when Carmelo Anthony returned from his injury, the team struggled, leading to Head Coach D'Antoni resigning. Landry Fields and Jeremy Lin were traded for some odd reason, and from then on, the Knicks would only peak at a .451 win rate and dipping as low as .207, the worst in franchise history. The 2020-21 season started off shaky but the Knicks, led by future Most Imporved Player Julius Randle, would once again make the playoffs only to lose to the balding Trae Young and his Atlanta Hawks.

While there were definitely some shining moments in Knicks history, there have been lengthy periods in which Knicks fans have been completely devoid of happiness.
